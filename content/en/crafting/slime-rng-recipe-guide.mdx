## The Fundamentals of Crafting in Slime RNG

Before hunting for scrolls, it is essential to understand how the crafting mechanic functions. Unlike standard rolls, crafting is a deterministic way to obtain specific units. Once you find a recipe scroll in the world, the "Crafting" menu at the central hub (or specific crafting stations) will update to show the required slimes.

Most recipes require a combination of three specific slimes. These can range from common "Rocky" slimes to ultra-rare pulls that have a 1 in a billion chance of appearing. 

> **Warning:** Crafting a unit consumes the required slimes from your inventory. Ensure you have "Auto-Delete" settings configured correctly so you don't accidentally trash a slime needed for a high-tier recipe.

## Mid-Game Recipe Locations (Zones 10 - 12)

As you push into the double-digit zones, the recipes become significantly harder to locate, often requiring a bit of platforming or careful observation of the environment.

### The Geode Unit (Mushroom Forest)
In the Mushroom Forest, players must navigate to the far left side of the zone near the boundary line. Look for a large red mushroom. You will need to jump and climb the cap of the mushroom to reach the scroll resting on top. 

| Unit Name | Location | Ingredients Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Geode** | Mushroom Forest | 1x **Ninja**, 1x **Guess**, 1x **Aegis** |

### Slime Slime Slime (Zone 12: Redwood Forest)
The Redwood Forest is filled with large logs and stumps, making it a maze for the unwary. To find this recipe, head to the far right side of the zone (facing forward toward the next area). Look for a hollow log. The scroll is tucked into a small pocket on the side of the log that faces away from the main path.

> **💡 Tip:** This unit is unique because it requires three of the same slime type, which can be difficult to accumulate if your inventory space is limited.

| Unit Name | Location | Ingredients Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Slime Slime Slime** | Redwood Forest | 3x **Slime Slime** |

The final tiers of crafting in Slime RNG involve slimes with incredibly low drop rates. Finding the scrolls is only 10% of the work; the remaining 90% is the grind for the ingredients.

### The Candy Unit (Zone 14: Candy Land)
Candy Land is visually overwhelming due to its bright colors. The recipe is located very close to the start of the zone. Look for the large "Oreo" structure and the adjacent candy bar. The scroll is hidden behind the candy bar on the right side.

| Unit Name | Location | Ingredients Required |

This is currently one of the most prestigious units to craft. Upon entering the Crystal Cavern, do not follow the ground path. Instead, look for a high ridge that runs along the center of the cavern. You will need to platform up to the very top of this ridge. The scroll sits in the middle of the elevated platform, before you reach the main crystal clusters.

| Unit Name | Location | Ingredients Required |

| **Crystal Unit** | Crystal Cavern | 1x **478M Rarity**, 1x **1.07B Rarity**, 1x **300M Rarity** |

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Slime RNG recipe guide scrolls?**

A: Scrolls are hidden throughout the world in specific zones. Common locations include behind vines in the Cave (Zone 6), on top of mushrooms in the Mushroom Forest, and on high ridges in the Crystal Cavern (Zone 16).

**Q: Do I need to find the scroll every time I want to craft a unit?**

A: No, once you have claimed a recipe scroll, it is permanently unlocked in your crafting menu. You only need to find it once to see the requirements and craft the unit as many times as you have ingredients for.

**Q: What happens if I can't find the scroll in the Alliance Zone?**

A: The Alliance Zone is a late-game area. If the scroll isn't visible, it may be hidden behind a destructible object or require a specific platforming sequence that is only accessible after reaching a certain level or roll count.

**Q: Are crafted slimes better than rolled slimes?**

A: Generally, yes. Crafted slimes like the Geode or the Crystal Unit often have unique visual effects and higher prestige value than standard rolls of similar rarity. They serve as a badge of honor for players who have explored the map thoroughly.
