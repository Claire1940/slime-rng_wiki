## Core Principle: Build Permanent Luck Before Burst Luck

The biggest mistake early and mid-game players make is overvaluing short buffs before building a stable baseline. Your first milestone should be permanent luck tiers in the upgrade menu, because those benefits keep working across every roll and every session.

Temporary boosts are still powerful, but they work best when layered on top of a high permanent base. Think of it like multipliers: stronger base stats make every timed buff more meaningful.

| Luck Source | Type | Duration | Priority | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Luck upgrade tiers** | Permanent | Always on | **Highest** | Improves all future rolls consistently |
| **Loot luck-boost upgrade** | Permanent unlock/support | Always on | **High** | Improves access to temporary luck items |
| **Consumable luck boosts** | Temporary | Short window | **Medium-High** | Great for focused roll bursts |
| **Random farming without upgrades** | None | N/A | **Low** | Inefficient coin-to-progress ratio |

> **Tip:** If you can afford either another permanent luck tier or one more random farm detour, buy the permanent tier first. Long-term value usually wins.

A clean progression model looks like this:
1. Push permanent luck tiers whenever affordable.  
2. Improve systems that increase luck boost drops.  
3. Save temporary boosts for planned farming windows.  
4. Repeat with better coin efficiency each cycle.

## Boost Timing, Stacking Logic, and Inventory Discipline

A smart **slime rng best luck strategy** is less about spamming items and more about timing pressure points correctly.

### Best timing windows
- Right after route reset when enemy density is high
- During uninterrupted play blocks (no errands, no queue breaks)
- When you already have momentum and quick clear speed

### Inventory rules
- Keep boost items organized and visible
- Avoid using a buff when inventory is nearly full
- Remove low-value clutter before a boost run

### Stacking mindset
Even when game systems vary by patch, the strategic rule is stable: prioritize permanent stats first, then layer temporary effects for burst value. If two temporary effects can overlap, test carefully in short runs before committing valuable items.

| Boost Use Scenario | Use Now or Save? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **You can play uninterrupted for full duration** | **Use now** | Maximum uptime and value |
| **You’re about to log off soon** | **Save** | Wastes active time |
| **Route is crowded/slow today** | **Usually save** | Lower kill rate reduces returns |
| **You just upgraded permanent luck** | **Use now** | New baseline amplifies short-term gains |

> **Warning:** Temporary luck feels strong, but relying on it without permanent upgrades creates inconsistent progress and bigger dry streak frustration.
