## Mastering the Slime Legion: Ring Attribute RNG

One of the most misunderstood aspects of the **Slime RNG slime list** ecosystem is the Slime Legion dungeon. This is where you craft rings that can provide massive percentage boosts to your attack power. However, the RNG involved in selecting attributes can be punishing if you don't know what to look for.

When running the Slime Legion, you are presented with various choices. The most important rule is to distinguish between text colors:
*   **Yellow Text:** These are permanent attributes that will stay on your ring once the dungeon run is completed.
*   **Blue Text:** These are temporary boosts that only apply to the current dungeon run. 

### Companion Damage vs. Skill Damage
A common mistake players make is prioritizing Skill Damage because the numbers often look larger. However, empirical testing in 2026 shows that Companion Damage consistently outperforms Skill Damage in high-level stages. For example, a ring with 1,400% Companion Damage has been shown to leave bosses with significantly less HP (approx. 82.9 AA) compared to a ring with 1,700% Skill Damage (approx. 136 AA).

| Ring Level | Ring Grade | Attack Boost % |

| 188 | **Grade D** | 13,000% |

As shown in the table above, the jump from Grade D to Grade S provides a nearly 40% total attack increase. This confirms that you should almost always prioritize "Increase Grade" options during your Slime Legion runs, even if it means skipping a decent damage attribute in the short term.

## Optimal Attribute Priority List

When you are rolling for your perfect ring to complement your **Slime RNG slime list** characters, follow this hierarchy of importance. This list assumes you are pushing for maximum stage progression rather than farming gold.

1.  **Ring Grade:** Always the top priority to ensure high base attack scaling.
2.  **Companion Damage:** The primary damage driver for the current meta.
3.  **Boss Damage:** Useful for overcoming stage walls, though it scales less effectively than Companion Damage.
4.  **Critical Hit Damage:** A secondary boost that provides marginal gains (usually around 2-5% total damage increase).
5.  **HP:** Only choose this if you are being "one-shot" by bosses and cannot survive long enough to deal damage.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid selecting "Gold Obtain" or "Evasion" on your primary combat rings. Gold rings should be crafted separately and only used when you are idling on a stage you can easily clear.

## Strategy: How to Roll the Perfect Ring

To get the most out of the Slime Legion RNG, you need a strategy for your refreshes. You have a limited number of refreshes per run, and using them at the right time is key to securing an S-Rank ring with high Companion Damage.

### Early Floor Strategy (Floors 1-5)
In the early stages of the dungeon, focus on securing at least one or two "Increase Grade" rolls. If the options are poor (e.g., Treasure or Skill Cooldown), use your refreshes here. It is better to risk an early refresh to get on the path to an S-Rank ring than to settle for mediocre stats that won't help you in the Kokytus leagues.

### Mid-to-Late Floor Strategy (Floors 6-15)
By floor 10, you should ideally be at Grade A or S. At this point, focus entirely on Companion Damage. Floor 5 and Floor 10 often offer higher potential rolls for damage attributes. If you see a Companion Damage roll above 200%, take it immediately.

## FAQ

**Q: Which slime from the Slime RNG slime list is best for F2P players?**

A: For Free-to-Play players, Serpine remains a top choice due to her high evasion capabilities, which allow you to survive longer against bosses that would otherwise one-shot you. However, always aim to save gems for SS-tier limited banners like Gentle.

**Q: How often should I replace my current ring?**

A: You don't need to replace your ring every time you level up. A high-quality level 120 ring with 1,500% Companion Damage is often better than a level 160 ring with poor attributes. Only swap when you find a ring that offers a significant Grade increase or a higher total Companion Damage percentage.

**Q: Does the "Gold Obtain" stat help with progression?**

A: Not directly. Gold is primarily used for leveling up basic stats which have diminishing returns in the late game. Your primary progression will come from the **Slime RNG slime list** upgrades, toys, and high-grade rings. Only use gold rings for offline farming.

**Q: Why is my S-Rank ring doing less damage than my A-Rank ring?**

A: This usually happens because the A-Rank ring has high Companion Damage attributes while the S-Rank ring is filled with "dead" stats like Skill Cooldown, Evasion, or Gold Obtain. Grade provides the base attack, but attributes provide the multipliers.
